# Chapter 15 — Surviving Drawdowns Without Emotional Collapse

## Goal
Equip readers to endure drawdowns—both financially and psychologically—so they can keep trading long enough for their edge to play out.

## Thesis
Drawdowns are inevitable; reducing size, tracking the right metrics, and protecting mental health prevent temporary pain from becoming permanent damage.

## Section Outline
### Opening — The six-month losing streak
- Personal low point and what almost broke you.

### Section 1 — Expectancy over win rate
- Why a string of losses doesn’t always mean a broken system.
- Calculating expectancy and variance realistically.

### Section 2 — Metrics that matter in drawdowns
- Max drawdown, time to recovery, risk of ruin.
- Journaling setups vs. outcomes to spot drift.

### Section 3 — When to reduce size or pause
- Rules for cutting position sizes during losing streaks.
- Criteria for stepping away entirely to protect mental state.

### Section 4 — Psychological tools
- Pre-mortems, meditation, accountability partners.
- Separating identity from P&L.

### Closing and transition
- Emphasize survival as success; the next chapters cover taking profits and compounding when the tide turns.

## Examples and Stories
- How a forced break stopped the bleeding and led to clearer thinking.
- A time when reducing size restored confidence and performance.

## Data/Research Targets
- Personal performance curves highlighting drawdowns and recoveries.
- Academic references on trader psychology and resilience.

## Drafting Notes
- Keep tone encouraging; normalize setbacks without sugarcoating.
